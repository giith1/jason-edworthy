What Should You Do If Youve Been Served a Party Wall Notice?

Receiving a Party Wall Notice means your neighbor is planning construction or renovation work that might impact your property. Its not just paperworkits an opportunity to ensure your propertys safety during the process.

What Kind of Work is Being Planned?

Common projects triggering a Party Wall Notice include basement or loft conversions, home extensions, or even internal changes like removing a chimney breast. The notice must outline the specific work your neighbor intends to carry out. Importantly, you have just 14 days to respondso dont sit on it.

Whats the Purpose of the Notice?

The Party Wall Act is designed to balance interests. It allows your neighbor to proceed with their construction while giving you a say in how its done. Crucially, it ensures a Schedule of Condition (SOC) is recorded for your property before work begins. Think of this as your propertys before snapshot to safeguard against disputes later.

Will You Be Protected?

The SOC is your safety net. If the work causes damage, it will be clear the building activities next door are responsible. The law ensures those undertaking the work cover the cost of repairs. Bonus: your neighbor usually foots the bill for the surveyor drafting the Party Wall Award, so youre not out of pocket.

Whats a Party Wall Agreement?

A Party Wall Agreement is a legal document that defines the rights and responsibilities of neighbors when construction or renovation occurs near a shared boundary. Its essential for projects like:

  • Digging foundations
  • Excavating for a basement
  • Building extensions connected to a shared wall

Key Benefits of a Party Wall Agreement:

  • Keeps your project on schedule and within the law.
  • Avoids unnecessary disputes by clearly defining responsibilities.
  • Ensures damages are addressed and repairs managed fairly.
